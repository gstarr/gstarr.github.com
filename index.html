Hi!

I'm a writer, designer, humorist, and data nerd. People sometimes pay me to “analyze” things and I make a lot of kind of cool things that I always forget to add to my portfolio. I'm interested in technology-driven applications to innovative and comprehensive storytelling, and also comedy.

I'm currently a Dow Jones News Fund Digital Media Intern with the Christian Science Monitor in Boston, where I cover technology for the Innovations section (and occasionally design cool things). With Dow Jones, I worked on a week-long multimedia project with other student journalists. In four days, the 14 of us created nine infographics, five podcasts, two videos, and four longform stories, wrapped into one "Phantom in Phoenix" multimedia project

For two years, I was Editor of The Zamboni, Tufts University's oldest student-run humor and satire magazine, where I redesigned the magazine's layout and print organization. I also established and built the magazine's first web presence and digital publishing.

I enjoy doing "serious" things like design and data analysis, and have been involved in roles that have words like "research" and "intern" in the title. I also do less serious things like comedy, and I write long-form articles and screenplays and run-on sentences where everything is in "quotes" and musicals and sometimes I build and design things with computers. If I have any free time, you can find me writing, designing, learning a new skill, making lists, rock climbing, or reading.

Feel free to connect with me on Twitter or LinkedIn, or see what I'm listening to on Last.fm. If you want, you can check out an (outdated) resume here or send me an email!
