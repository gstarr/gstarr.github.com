Hi!

I'm a writer, designer, journalist, and humorist with experience in technical and analytical fields. I am often hired to “analyze” things and I make a lot of kind of cool things that I always forget to add to my currently-under-construction portfolio (for published works, click here). I also do comedy and humor things and am interested in creative and innovative approaches to comprehensive storytelling.

This summer, I was a Dow Jones Digital Media Fellow with the award-winning Christian Science Monitor in Boston. There, I wrote stories for the Technology and Innovations desks, and served as the mobile and tablet lead on the website's feature redesign. After my fellowship ended, I was kept on as a freelance designer.

In May, the Dow Jones News Fund sent me to a bootcamp program at the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication at Arizona State University with the other digital media fellows. There, we were trained in AP style and multimedia production, and worked on a week-long, intensive, digitally-integrated multimedia project: "Phantom in Phoenix." In four days, the 14 of us created nine infographics, five podcasts, two videos, and four longform stories, wrapped into one comprehensive project.

Before that, I was Editor-in-Chief of The Zamboni, Tufts University's premiere humor and satire magazine, where I redesigned the magazine's layout and print organization and managed its 50-person staff. I also established and built the magazine's first digital publishing platform and grew readership by more than 600%.

Before all of those things, for three and a half years, I worked in cyber security and national security consulting.

I'm really interested in experimental approaches to media, bridging comedy, technology, and journalism in positive ways. If you'd like to talk, see my work, or hire me, feel free to reach out at any time.

You can connect with me on Twitter or LinkedIn, or see what I'm listening to on Last.fm. If you want, you can check out my (somewhat outdated) resume here or send me an email!
