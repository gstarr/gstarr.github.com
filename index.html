Hi!

I'm Graham Starr, a writer, designer, journalist, and humorist who makes things with computers and has experience in technical and analytical fields. I am often hired to “analyze” or "build" things and I make a lot of kind of cool things that I always forget to add to my constantly-under-construction portfolio. I'm interested in creative and innovative approaches to comprehensive storytelling. Currently, I work on Projects at The Atlantic.

Before I was at The Atlantic, I was a media consultant at the UN Refugee Agency. Before that, I was a Dow Jones Digital Intern with the Christian Science Monitor, where I wrote about technology for the Innovations desk, and served as the mobile lead on the website's feature redesign.

Last year, the Dow Jones News Fund sent me to a bootcamp program at the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism and Mass Communication at Arizona State University with the other digital media interns. There, we were trained in AP style and multimedia production, and worked on a week-long, intensive, digitally-integrated multimedia project: "Phantom in Phoenix." In four days, the 14 of us created nine infographics, five podcasts, two videos, and four longform stories, wrapped into one comprehensive project.

Before I was a professional human being, I was the Editor-in-Chief of The Zamboni, Tufts University's premiere humor and satire magazine, where I revived the publication, redesigned the magazine's layout and print organization, and managed its 60-person staff. I also established and built The Zamboni's first digital publishing platform and grew readership and staff by more than 600%.

I'm really interested in experimental approaches to media, bridging comedy, technology, and journalism in positive ways. If you'd like to talk, see my work, or hire me to do something cool for you, feel free to email me at any time.

You can also connect with me on Twitter or LinkedIn, or see what I'm listening to on Last.fm.
