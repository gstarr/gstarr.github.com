Hi! I'm Graham Starr.

I'm a senior at Tufts University, pursuing a double major in International Relations and Physics, with a minor in Linguistics. My interests concern innovation theory, specifically its effect in the establishment of multidisciplinary fields, and the ways in which we can apply science and technology to international affairs most effectively. I am also interested in critical theory, literary criticism, technology in development and innovation, and network theory. I have experience with design, journalism, marketing, cyber security, web development, operational strategy, and data science, among other things, and hope to combine all of these things in some capacity in the future.

I'm currently the Editor-in-Chief of The Zamboni, Tufts' oldest student-run humor and satire magazine. I was previously Managing Editor, where I redesigned the magazine's layout and print organization, and ran Art Direction and web presence. If I have any free time, you can find me writing, designing, learning a new skill, making lists, rock climbing, or reading.

Feel free to connect with me on Twitter or LinkedIn, or see what I'm listening to on Last.fm. If you want, you can check out my resume here or send me an email!

I also have a portfolio!
